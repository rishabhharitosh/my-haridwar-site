Significance of Maha Shivratri

Maha Shivratri is one of the most important Hindu festivals, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It symbolizes the divine marriage of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati and is believed to be the night when Lord Shiva performed the cosmic dance (Tandava).

On Shivratri, devotees observe fasting, chant Shiva mantras and perform special pujas to seek blessings for peace, prosperity and spiritual growth.

Why Rudrabhishek Is Performed on Shivratri

Rudrabhishek is a powerful Vedic ritual in which Lord Shiva is worshipped with milk, water, honey, curd, bilva leaves and sacred mantras.

  • Brings peace, health and prosperity
  • Helps fulfill wishes and removes obstacles
  • Provides spiritual upliftment and inner strength
